What does it mean to Think Like a Founder?

Hadar begins at the beginning. A frontier academy where promising young Jews can draw from their roots and emerge fully formed. We study right from the primary source so our students can appreciate the great chain of Western achievement that connects Abraham to Socrates to Washington to Ben-Gurion. To learn like a founder, speak like a founder, and think like a founder.

The Three Pillars

Our Three Pillars

I Pillar One

Jewish

"And you shall teach them diligently to your children." — Deuteronomy 6:7

Hadar students receive an Orthodox Jewish education rooted in the enduring traditions of our people. Through Hebrew immersion, Torah study, prayer, and song, our students develop linguistic and spiritual fluency. Hadar seeks to root its students in a sense of belonging, to instill a sense of responsibility in passing forward the torch of our faith.

II Pillar Two

Classical

"The object of education is to teach us what is worthy of being loved." — Plato

Hadar's curriculum is rooted in the greatest works of the Western Canon. Students learn classical languages in order to engage directly with the linguistic foundations of civilization itself. Through Socratic discussion, Hadar educates rather than indoctrinates, encouraging students to think deeply about the ideas that formed the West.

III Pillar Three

American

"Liberty cannot be preserved without a general knowledge among the people." — John Adams

Hadar reminds its students of America's biblical origins. That Leviticus is inscribed on the Liberty Bell and that our Constitution derives from our Commandments. Students study the intellectual arc that spans from Jerusalem to Athens to Washington, inspiring our students to graduate from the Academy and go on to serve their country.

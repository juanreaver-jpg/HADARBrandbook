— 01 / Manifesto

The Founders

America's future rests on uncertain foundations. Our institutions have lost credibility, and the promise of a stable career may soon be a thing of the past. Lacking true moral direction, our schools bury the lede of their declining outcomes by implementing flashy initiatives. But while education-as-a-service looks good on a brochure, it fails in the crafting of the soul. Students learn to speak in content, but few are fluent in character.

Amidst this uncertainty, America has forgotten its biblical origins. It has forgotten that the Liberty Bell is inscribed with the words of Leviticus, and that our Constitution was inspired by the Commandments handed down to Moses. Besieged by gale-force nihilistic winds, our people and institutions no longer derive meaning from the source. Old truths must break new ground.

Hadar begins at the beginning. A frontier academy for the future's prime movers. A place where promising young Jews can rise from their roots and emerge as a moral people. Our students come to appreciate the great chain of Western achievement that connects Abraham to Socrates to Washington to Ben-Gurion. To learn like a founder, speak like a founder, and think like a founder.

Hadar students study both Hebrew and Latin, granting them direct access to the foundational texts of the Canon. Our students don't take shortcuts. They sound out each syllable of truth. To become multilingual is also to become a better critical thinker, and thus better equipped to serve covenant and constitution. Hadar is a place for principled pioneers, for the founders of the future to learn from the founders of the past.

— 08 / Tone of Voice

How We Speak.

Four dimensions that shape our voice across all communication.

Reverent

Amidst a culture of nihilism and bar charts, Hadar believes in educating the soul. Hadar speaks with reverence, reflecting a commitment to moral and spiritual truth over robotic optimization. Hadar is a place to learn like the founders, not to become a mindlessly efficient machine.

We don't say:
"Learn 2x in 2hrs. Alpha classes learn twice as fast as their peers and rank in the top 1% nationwide." (Alpha School)

This reduces education to metrics and time-slots. Parents will hear measurement but not meaning.

We do say:
"A place where promising young Jews can rise from their roots and emerge as a moral people."

Describes students not as rootless economic units, but members of an eternal tradition. Still commits to emergence and the fulfillment of their potential.

Confident

Hadar speaks with clarity and confidence. We are a school that practices Orthodox Judaism, and aren't afraid to say it. Our language should feel specific and honest rather than vague, "inclusive," or overly accommodating.

We don't say:
"Austin Jewish Academy is a 100-student, K-8, co-ed, pluralistic Jewish Day School." (Austin Jewish Academy)

This sounds weak and ideologically noncommittal. "Pluralistic" feels like a cop-out that avoids having to say what the school actually believes.

We do say:
"Receive an Orthodox Jewish education rooted in classical rigor and American ideals."

Clearly states our commitment to Orthodox Judaism, classics, and national pride.

Down-to-Earth

Hadar is religious without being judgemental or pious. Our speech and language should feel down-to-Earth rather than saccharine or grandiose.

We don't say:
"As the Lord's regents, we are called to be caretakers of all that He has placed in our hands." (Regent's School of Austin)

This sounds pretentious and intimidating. Parents don't need to hear sermons, they need an honest statement of the values their children will be taught.

We do say:
"Amidst this uncertainty, America has forgotten its biblical origins. It has forgotten that the Liberty Bell is inscribed with the words of Leviticus, and that our Constitution was inspired by the Commandments handed down to Moses."

Still inspiring and powerful without lapsing into theatrics. Connects spirituality and moral education to the real world and American history.

Traditional

Hadar believes strong educational communities are built through timeless values and moral wisdom, not vague emotional pandering. Warmth should be shown, not told through therapeutic and sentimental messaging.

We don't say:
"To create a warm, welcoming, and supportive faith community where members can connect, explore, and support each other on our shared Jewish journey." (Temple Beth Shalom)

Words like welcoming and supportive feel vague and emotionally insincere. Phrasing like Jewish journey trivializes the seriousness and gravity of our faith.

We do say:
"Hadar is a place for principled pioneers, for the founders of the future to learn from the founders of the past."

This language emphasizes continuity and inheritance. It frames education as the passing of an eternal flame rather than a vague emotional experience. Roots the education in everlasting and unmalleable truths.

— 09 / Art Direction

Chromolithography
as Principle.

Layered, vivid, and historically grounded. A printed, collected feel — rich color, visible depth, and a sense of cultural memory rather than slick digital polish.

For Hadar, chromolithography helps bridge old and new: the language of the past stays present, but alive, sweeping, and full of movement. Imagery is not decoration — it is doctrine made visible.

10.1

Three guiding principles.

Every image — photographic or illustrated — should be tested against all three. Drop any that fails.

01

Contrast

Old vs new. A modern child's hands on a centuries-old text. The tension between living youth and inherited tradition is the core visual idea.

02

Immersion

Students are not just looking at tradition — they're inside it. Sweeping, full-scene compositions immerse the viewer in old languages, history, and atmosphere.

03

Action

The scenes feel alive. Movement, gesture, and participation — students learning through doing, not static posing. The history is active, not decorative.

10.2

Two image categories.

Hadar's visual system runs on two image types. Each does specific work; together they hold the brand.

A

Photographs of students.

Documentary · Present tense

Real children at the school — studying, praying, playing, debating. Natural light, candid moments, hands on real books. The proof that this is happening, now.

  • Available alone — no historical pairing needed.
  • Faces in concentration, not performance.
  • Warm tones, no heavy filters or cool digital edits.
B

Founder illustrations.

Sweeping · Past tense

Lone historical scenes — prophets, founders, scholars — rendered in full chromolithograph richness. No modern figures required. These set the moral and cultural backdrop.

  • Available alone — historical figure carries the frame.
  • Sweeping landscape compositions, never tight portraits.
  • Full chromolithograph palette: crimson, lapis, antique gold, manuscript green.
10.3

Gallery.

Twenty-one plates · Three categories
— Founder illustrations

Lone historical figures and sweeping scenes. Full chromolithograph palette, no modern child in the frame — these carry the weight themselves.

— Tools of Learning

Seven anchors and three pillars · The full curriculum. Objects that anchor each subject of the Hadar curriculum — every domain rendered as a single emblem — set among the three pillars: menorah, column, and the Liberty Bell.

— Student life photography

The actual classroom, the actual day. Unposed photography of the Hadar room at work — proof, not promise.

— Direction notes
  • ALone historical illustrations and lone student photographs each stand on their own — no pairing required.
  • BCompositions sweep across the frame — no centered, static portraits.
  • CColor is restrained but rich — crimson, lapis, antique gold, manuscript green. Never neon, never flat.
  • DTexture is welcome — grain, paper edges, foxed margins. Slick digital surfaces are not Hadar.

— Mark rules
A

One color

Marks are always rendered in a single color from the approved palette. No gradients, no two-color treatments, no drop shadows.

B

Clear space

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the crown around the mark on every side. Nothing crops or crowds the crest.

C

Minimum size

Primary crest: 32px digital · 0.5in print. Below this, switch to the jeweled crest, which holds together at smaller sizes.

D

Never recolor parts

Do not isolate or recolor individual elements (crown, gems, menorah). The mark is one mark.

— 10.5 / Brand System

Lockup Variations.

The HADAR identity has one canonical mark and two families that radiate from it. The canonical lockup — Stacked, "Think Like a Founder" — is the single full application of the logo. The supporting marks adapt that crest for navigation, ceremonial, and tagline-less contexts. The simplified versions reduce the crest to its essential elements for the most constrained applications. Every mark maintains clear space and minimum sizing to preserve legibility and brand integrity.

Canonical Mark
Variant 01 — Stacked · Think Like a Founder
The one canonical, full application of the HADAR logo. Full crest above wordmark with the "Think Like a Founder" tagline. This is the default mark — reach for it first for formal documents, print, cover pages, and any brand-forward touchpoint. Every other variation is a derivative for a specific constraint.

Supporting Marks

Derivatives of the canonical lockup for layouts where the full stacked mark or tagline doesn't fit — horizontal arrangements, tagline-less crests, lettermarks, and the ceremonial seal.

Variant 02 — Horizontal · Think Like a Founder
Primary logo, horizontal arrangement. Crest left, wordmark and "Think Like a Founder" tagline right. Minimum width: 280px. Use for headers, email signatures, merchandise, and wide-format layouts.
Variant 03 — Wordmark · Think Like a Founder
Primary logo, wordmark with tagline, no crest. Use for tight digital layouts or secondary placements where the full crest would compete with surrounding content.
Variant 04 — Crest Mark · Stacked
Supporting mark. Large crest with wordmark, no tagline. Hero configuration for ceremonial contexts — award certificates, letterheads, signage, and official school documents.
Variant 05 — Crest Mark · Horizontal
Supporting mark. Compact horizontal lockup — crest with wordmark only, no tagline. Minimum width: 160px. Use for navigation bars, web headers, and digital contexts requiring a clean horizontal mark.
Variant 06 — Lettermark
Supporting mark. Name only — no crest, no tagline. Minimum width: 120px. Use for watermarks, repeated patterns, or strictly space-constrained contexts.
Variant 07 — Lettermark · Reversed
Supporting mark. Vellum-colored lettermark for dark backgrounds. Use on Lapis Blue, Deep Ink, or photography where the standard mark would lose contrast.
Variant 08 — Radiant Crest · Ceremonial Seal
Supporting mark. The crest in its fullest, most ornamental form: crowned frame haloed by a sunburst of rays. No wordmark — pure mark. Reserved for ceremonial and emblematic use: diplomas, awards, wax seals, embossed covers, watermarks, and the brand's hero crest placement.

Simplified Versions

A reduced family from the latest logo package, built for the most constrained applications — minimum sizing, embroidery, stamping, and single-color reproduction. Here the crest is pared back to its essential elements, and individual pieces (the crown, the rays) may stand on their own. Use these only where the canonical or supporting marks would lose detail or legibility.

Simplified 01 — Solid Crown · Stacked
Simplified mark. The crown reduced to a solid silhouette, stacked above the wordmark. Use at small sizes and in single-color reproduction where the detailed crest would fill in or blur.
Simplified 02 — Solid Crown · Horizontal
Simplified mark. Solid-silhouette crown beside the wordmark for tight horizontal spaces — app bars, favicons with a label, narrow merchandise tags, and embroidered patches.
Simplified 03 — Solid Crown · Standalone
Simplified mark, no wordmark. The crown as one solid shape — the most reduced emblem in the system. Ideal for embroidery, stamping, app icons, and favicons at very small sizes.
Simplified 04 — Crest · Simplified Rays
Simplified mark. The crown framed by a pared-back sunburst — fewer, cleaner rays than the ceremonial seal. Holds the ornamental feel while staying crisp at reduced sizes and in single-color print.
Simplified 05 — Crown Frame · No Rays
Simplified mark. The crown frame on its own, rays removed. A clean emblem for embossing, stationery corners, and contexts where the sunburst would crowd surrounding elements.
Simplified 06 — Crown · Standalone
Simplified mark. The crown element used entirely on its own — the smallest standalone piece of the crest. Reserved for accents, bullet ornaments, embroidered detailing, and decorative repeats.
